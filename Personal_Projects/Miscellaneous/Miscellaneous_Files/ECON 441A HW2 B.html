Question 3F¶

In initial outlier analysis, a major concern is runaway outlier removal. Each successive sweep for outliers adjusts the definition of an outlier within the dataset, which then results in previously "normal" observations being dubbed outliers and marked for removal. Fortunately, this can be easily solved by setting a threshold value prior to any removals and only performing the initial removal.

Another concern is the removal of a natural and legitimate observation under the guise of outlier removal, which can be seen in the case of an unlikely win by a political candidate. By removing such an observation, you could reduce the predictive strength of the regression and — for example — overestimate the effect of a candidate being mainstream on their chances of electoral success. Avoiding this situation requires a thorough understanding of the dataset being scanned for outliers.
